Consistent Framework

The Same Methodology, Five Times

Every project follows a five-stage evaluation pipeline. The framework ensures that production decisions are based on evidence — not model preference or seniority of the analyst who ran the last notebook.

1
Baseline First
Establish and measure the naive / rule-based method before evaluating anything complex
P1P2P3P4P5
2
Model Building
Evaluate all candidate methods with at least two independent metrics and proper temporal validation
P1P2P3P4P5
3
Robustness Testing
Re-run with different seeds, time windows, and hyperparameter values to confirm stability
P1P2P4P5
4
Cost-Benefit Analysis
Quantify compute, maintenance, FP investigation cost, and incremental value vs baseline
P1P2P3P4P5
5
Production Decision
A written recommendation with one clear decision, one key number, and conditions for reversal
P1P2P3P4P5

In three of five projects, the recommended method was more complex than the baseline — because the data supported it. In two projects, a simpler method was co-deployed or chosen over a technically superior alternative because the operational cost of the complex method outweighed its incremental benefit. This is what production-grade data science looks like.

Exact TSP was rejected despite being provably optimal: incremental saving over 2-Opt is 1.75% of $2.45M = ~$43K/year, but the monthly cost difference is $450 − $1.55 = $448 × 12 = $5,376/year.
